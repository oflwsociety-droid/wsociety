The 6 deliverables
What every member actually gets.
These are not promises. These are the six things we are building, testing, and running every single cycle. Each one has an owner. Each one has a standard. If it doesn't deliver real value, it gets fixed or cut.
01
Weekly War Room
Every Sunday, 90 minutes. One member presents a live problem — a deal stuck, a pitch that failed, a business not moving, a decision they can't make. The group's job is not to be nice. It's to be useful. Every attendee gets assigned actions before the call ends. You leave with decisions, not discussions.
Sundays · 90 min · Mandatory
02
Skill Sprints
Biweekly 2-week challenges assigned to the full group. Cold outreach 5 businesses and close one. Build a one-page pitch for a real idea. Run a discovery call. Shadow a negotiation. Each Sprint has a clear objective, a 14-day window, and a specific deliverable. Output is required — not optional, not "I tried."
Biweekly · Assigned Monday · Output required
03
Member Dossiers
Every member maintains a live one-pager: current ventures, 90-day goals, what you need from the room, what you've shipped recently. Updated at the start of every month. This forces clarity on where you actually are. It also exposes — quickly — who is moving and who is stagnating. There is nowhere to hide.
Monthly update · Shared with all members
04
W Library
A curated stack of books, frameworks, case studies, and decision models — mapped to the five member archetypes: Strategist, Dealmaker, Builder, Arguer, Closer. Not a recommendation list. A curriculum. Monthly reading with a structured discussion. The goal is not to read more — it's to think better and faster than the people around you outside this room.
Monthly reading · Archetype-mapped · Discussed live
05
The Deal Board
An internal board for member-to-member opportunities. Client overflow you can't take on, partnership needs, pilot users required, co-founder searches, referrals worth money. First right of refusal goes to W Society members before anything goes external. The Board creates real economic value between members — not just conversation.
Always open · Members-only access
06
Monthly Verdict
Every member is scored monthly on the W Score across four verticals: Earn, Build, Show, Commit. Your score is private. Your Verdict is collective — the group sees Green, Yellow, or Red. Two consecutive Red months triggers a removal review. This is not a threat. It's the mechanism that keeps the room worth being in. No passengers. Ever.
Monthly · W Score driven · Non-negotiable
Operating rhythm
The cadence that keeps
everything moving.
W Society runs on a fixed rhythm. Not when people feel like it. Not when it's convenient. This is the operating calendar — memorise it, protect it.
Weekly
War Room — Sunday, 8pm, 90 minutes
One presenter. The presenter submits their problem in writing 24 hours before the call — no cold drops. Full group attends. Actions are assigned before the call ends. Attendance is tracked. One miss triggers a yellow flag.
Biweekly
Skill Sprint drop + debrief
Every second Monday, a new Sprint is assigned. On the same call, the previous Sprint is debriefed — members share their outputs, the group evaluates. No output = noted. Pattern of no output = flagged.
Monthly
W Score review + dossier update
All members submit updated dossiers at the start of every month. W Score is calculated across Earn, Build, Show, and Commit. Verdicts are issued privately. The group sees aggregate status — Green, Yellow, or Red — for every member.
Quarterly
Intake window + deal review
Four times a year, the membership gate opens for new applicants. Existing members vote. Simultaneously, open deals on the Deal Board are reviewed for progress. Stale deals are closed. Active ones are escalated.
Annual
W Summit — in person, Bangalore
Full group, in the same room. Portfolio review — every member presents what they built this year. New member inauguration. Top performer of the year is recognised. Deals are closed in the room. This is the event the rest of the year earns the right to attend.

4-week build sprint
Before we sell it,
we build it right.
The five of us have four weeks to build every deliverable to a standard we'd each pay for. This is not planning. This is construction. Every role has a deadline. Every deliverable has a review. Nothing ships until it's undeniable.
01
Week one
Draft — every role builds their v1
  • Each role delivers a v1 doc of their deliverable by day 7
  • Format is written — not slides. Enough detail that a stranger understands the value
  • The only standard: "would a potential member see this and know this is serious?"
  • No group calls this week. Just build.
02
Week two
Cross-review — tear everything apart
  • Everyone reviews everyone else's deliverable — written feedback within 48 hours
  • Three questions per review: what's missing, what's weak, what's exceptional
  • Group call at end of week — 90 minutes, synthesise all feedback live
  • Ahmed makes final calls on what gets revised vs what stays as-is
03
Week three
Revise + dry run
  • Each role revises their deliverable based on cross-review feedback
  • Mock War Room — all five in the room, one person presents a real problem
  • Run one Skill Sprint brief internally — does it generate real output?
  • Fill in the dossier template as if you're a new member joining today
04
Week four
Polish + member welcome kit
  • Final refinements across all deliverables — bar is "would I pay ₹2,500 for this day one?"
  • Produce the member welcome kit: everything a new member receives when they join
  • Gate process confirmed, pricing locked, first intake list of 4–5 names drafted
  • Week 5: first applications go through the real process, not a mock
Collaboration protocol
How the five of us
work together.
This is not about being comfortable with each other. It's about building something exceptional together. These are the rules of the build phase. No exceptions.
I
Async first — everything documented
Every decision, revision, and deliverable lives in the shared workspace. No verbal-only agreements. If a decision was made in conversation and not written down within 24 hours, it didn't happen. Documentation is not bureaucracy — it's accountability.
II
48-hour review window — hard deadline
When someone shares a deliverable for review, every other member has 48 hours to give written feedback. Miss the window and you lose input rights on that decision. No chasing. No extensions. The window exists so work moves — not so people can get around to it when they feel like it.
III
Owner decides — Ahmed has veto
Each role has final say on their deliverable after incorporating group feedback. Ahmed can veto anything that doesn't meet the W Society standard. This is not a democracy. It's a meritocracy with clear accountability. Disagreement is welcome. Insubordination isn't a concept here — but neither is blind consensus.
IV
No "almost done" — submit or flag early
By day 7 of Week 1, you either have a v1 or you don't. "Almost done" is not a status. If you're going to miss a deadline, you flag it by day 5 — not day 8. This is how we treat each other's time. The group cannot plan around ambiguity.
V
The bar: "would I pay for this?"
Every deliverable is tested against one question. If any of the five founding members would hesitate to pay ₹2,500 per month for what we've built, it isn't ready. We are the first members. We are the test case. We cannot sell something we wouldn't buy.
Membership gate
How people get in
after we're done building.
The gate is what makes the room worth being in. It is not optional. It is not flexible. Every future member goes through every step — regardless of who they know or how they were referred.
Step 1
Referral or Radar — not public applications
The application window is not publicly open. You are either referred by an existing member or identified by the founding team. Cold DMs and unsolicited applications don't move. This is not gatekeeping for its own sake — it is the first filter.
Step 2
Written application — four questions, no filler
What are you building right now? What's a decision you made in the last 30 days that cost you something? What do you bring to a room of serious people? Why W Society? The questions are designed to be unanswerable with vague language. Either you have answers or you don't.
Step 3
30-minute call — conversation, not a pitch
The applicant meets Ahmed or Zaid. The question is not whether they can talk — it is how they think when the conversation goes somewhere uncomfortable. Preparation can fake polish. It cannot fake how someone reasons under pressure.
Step 4
Member vote
All current members see the application summary and call notes. Unanimous approval required for the first cohort. Once the group reaches 10+, a two-thirds majority is sufficient. Every member has a stake in who enters the room — because they live with the consequences either way.
Step 5
30-day probation
You're in conditionally. One War Room miss, one Sprint skipped, one dossier late — you're reviewed before full membership is confirmed. Full status is granted at day 31 only if you showed up. The probation is not a formality. It is the final filter.
